# Purpose

This document describes how a traveller experiences Travel Cockpit throughout an entire journey.

The focus is on the real travel experience instead of individual screens.

---

# Philosophy

Travel Cockpit is designed around one central idea:

> The traveller should spend time enjoying the journey instead of searching for information.

The application adapts to the phase of the trip.

---

# Phase 1 – Before Departure

The traveller prepares only the essentials.

Typical actions:

- Create a trip
- Add accommodations
- Invite travel companions
- Add tickets (optional)
- Add a few must-see activities (optional)

No detailed planning is required.

---

# Phase 2 – Arrival

The traveller arrives at the accommodation.

Travel Cockpit automatically focuses on the active accommodation.

The dashboard immediately shows:

- Current accommodation
- Weather
- Planned activities
- AI Suggestions
- Restaurants
- Cafés
- Energy Points
- Tickets

---

# Phase 3 – Exploring

This is the most important phase.

The traveller decides what to do today or during the coming days.

Typical actions:

- Ask AI for ideas
- Browse the map
- Search nearby
- Compare activities
- Add activities to planning

The application supports the traveller without taking control.

---

# Phase 4 – During Activities

The application becomes a practical travel companion.

Examples:

- Start Google Maps
- Open Komoot
- Open tickets
- Find a café
- Find charging or fuel
- View weather
- Mark activity as completed

Everything should require one tap.

---

# Phase 5 – Moving to the Next Accommodation

The application automatically shifts its focus.

The next accommodation becomes the new starting point.

Previous days remain available but are hidden by default.

---

# Phase 6 – End of the Trip

The traveller can review the journey.

Examples:

- Completed activities
- Visited accommodations
- Notes
- Photos

No social media timeline is generated.

The trip remains a personal travel archive.

---

# AI Throughout the Journey

AI is available in every phase.

AI can:

- Suggest activities
- Suggest restaurants
- Suggest cafés
- Suggest energy points
- Explain why something is recommended
- Compare alternatives
- Suggest rain alternatives

AI never:

- Adds activities automatically
- Removes activities
- Changes planning
- Changes data

Every AI suggestion requires confirmation.

---

# Design Principle

Every screen should answer one question:

> "What is useful for me right now?"

If a screen contains information that is not useful at that moment, it should be simplified.
